Абетка is a typeface built from the Ukrainian alphabet — where every letter carries a person, a place, an object, or an event that shaped the country's story. This is the archive behind the type.

Every glyph is a small monument.

A typeface is usually judged by its curves and counters. Абетка is judged by what it remembers. Each letterform in this specimen is paired with a fragment of Ukrainian history — some celebrated, some painful, some almost forgotten — so that typing the alphabet becomes an act of recollection.

Not an alphabet chart. A specimen of memory.

Абетка began as a type-design exercise and became something closer to an oral history, rendered in letterforms. Each entry follows the same rule: one letter, one sound, one story — told in a single paragraph, so it can be read in the time it takes to trace the glyph.

The typeface itself borrows its weight from Soviet-era signage and its warmth from folk embroidery patterns — heavy where history is heavy, and lighter where the story turns to song.
